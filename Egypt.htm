       Bedouins: These are nomadic tribes primarily found in the Sinai Peninsula and the Western Desert. They have a rich cultural heritage and are known for their traditional lifestyle, which includes camel herding and

 

       Nubians: Located mainly in southern Egypt along the Nile, Nubians have a distinct language and culture. They are known for their vibrant music, dance, and colorful houses.

 

       Berbers (Amazigh): Though fewer in number compared to other North African countries, Berbers in Egypt are primarily found in the Siwa Oasis. They have their own language and traditions

 

 

       Copts: The Copts are the largest Christian minority in Egypt, with a rich history dating back to the early days of Christianity. They have their own language, Coptic, which is used mainly in religious contexts.

 

       Beja Tribes: These tribes, including the Ababda and Bisharin, are found in the southeastern part of Egypt. They have a nomadic lifestyle and are known for their unique customs and traditions.

Egypt is well known for its delicious food, and some of these dishes are Koshary, Tamiaa, Ful medames, and Basbosa.

the koshary:

It is a traditional Egyptian dish that mainly includes:  elbow macaroni, rice, lentils, fried onions, chickpeas, tomato juice, vinegar,

It is a really popular dish, and it is full of vegetarian protein due to the chickpeas and lentils it contains. Therefore, it is cheap, and Christians can have it when they Fast too!

Tamiaa:

It is a popular dish made of beans. It is made in the form of disks using beans and vegetables past fried, and it is mainly eaten at breakfasts and dinners with or without bread.

Ful medames:
